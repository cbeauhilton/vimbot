How does this work?

This tool uses advanced AI technology called Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to provide answers to your queries based on the content of the Vanderbilt Internal Medicine Residency Handbook (vimbook.org).

Here's how RAG works:

  1. You enter a query related to internal medicine.
  2. The selected AI model processes your query and retrieves relevant passages from the VIMBook using a specialized retrieval system.
  3. The retrieved passages are then passed to the language model, which generates a response by combining the relevant information from the passages.
  4. The generated response is displayed on the screen for you to read and use.

Pros of RAG:

  • It allows the AI model to access and use specific information from the VIMBook to provide more accurate and relevant responses.
  • The retrieval step helps the model focus on the most pertinent information, leading to more targeted and efficient answer generation.

Cons of RAG:

  • Though it is less likely than it would be without the RAG, the underlying model can still hallucinate (create incorrect answers).
  • The quality of the responses depends on the accuracy and comprehensiveness of the retrieval system. If the retrieval system fails to find the most relevant passages, the generated response may be less accurate or incomplete.
  • RAG systems can be computationally expensive and may require more resources (and run more slowly) compared to standalone language models.

Despite the limitations, RAG is a powerful approach that combines the strengths of retrieval systems and language models to provide informative and context-aware responses.
